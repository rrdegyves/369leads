# Research notes: refunds, chargebacks, retainers (agencies)

*Not legal advice. Have a lawyer in your state review the final agreement.*

## What common agency terms do

- **Non-refundable for work in the billing period** — fees cover services rendered that month (planning, ads, optimization, reporting), not “leads sold” as a retail product.  
- **Dispute path before chargeback** — require written notice + cure period; state that initiating a chargeback for bona fide charges is a **breach** and may incur **chargeback fees** and collection.  
- **Guarantees as credit** — “50% off month 2” structured as **invoice credit / account credit** reduces clawback risk vs refund-to-card.  
- **Minimum term** — e.g. 2 months aligns with “week 1 build, weeks 2–4 optimize” so clients can’t exit before a fair test.  
- **ACH / bank debit** — some operators prefer ACH over cards for lower dispute rates; still document terms clearly.  
- **Clear scope** — vague “I didn’t get jobs” disputes are reduced when **qualified lead** is defined and **you don’t guarantee closed revenue or insurance outcomes**.

## Sources reviewed (patterns, not endorsement)

- Industry blogs and public agency **Terms / Refund** pages: recurring billing non-refundable for period started; chargebacks as breach; contact-us-first clauses.  
- Card network rules: claiming “service not received” is easier for buyers if you didn’t document delivery; **timestamps, CRM exports, ad screenshots, and call logs** support representment.

## Operational chargeback defense (as important as contract)

- Save **Meta invoices**, **lead export CSVs**, **email/Loom** proving weekly management.  
- Client **login** to GHL showing leads = evidence of delivery.  
- Respond to chargebacks fast with a **cover letter + exhibit list**.
