# Client offboarding, data export & handoff (SOP)

Use when a client **pauses**, **terminates**, or **downgrades**. Aligns with 2026 expectations for **professional** agencies and supports **chargeback defense** (clean paper trail).

---

## 1. Triggers

- Contract end / notice period complete  
- Non-payment after cure period  
- Mutual exit  
- Strategic fire (bad-fit client)

---

## 2. Within 5 business days of end date

**Agency**

- [ ] Send **final report**: leads delivered by month, CPL trend, top creatives, final spend summary  
- [ ] Export **lead CSV** (name, phone, email, date, source) from GHL → deliver via **secure link** or agreed channel  
- [ ] Confirm **who owns:** ad account, pixel, domain, landing (per contract—usually client owns their assets; you revoke **your** user access)  
- [ ] Remove **your BM access** to client assets after handoff (unless retained for another scope)  
- [ ] Send **closing invoice** or **credit note** if applicable  
- [ ] Archive **internal** evidence: final ads screenshot, export logs, Loom links (for disputes)

**Client**

- [ ] Change **passwords** / remove seats as needed  
- [ ] Point domain DNS **away** from your hosting if they leave  
- [ ] Fund their own **ad account** going forward

---

## 3. Data & privacy (benchmark practice)

- Retain **billing + delivery** records per your accountant/lawyer (often **years**).  
- **Homeowner PII:** do not keep unnecessary copies; delete **redundant** exports after handoff if policy allows.  
- If you act as **processor**, a short **DPA** clause in MSA helps (see audit doc).

---

## 4. Reputation

- **No public drama.** If asked for a reference, stay factual.  
- If client was **high churn risk**, note **ICP** lessons in your CRM.

---

## 5. Win-back (optional)

- 90-day **check-in** email: “If you want territory back, reply **REOPEN**.”
