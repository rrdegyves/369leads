# SOP: Meta “Special Ad Category” for roofing / homeowner lead gen

**Purpose:** Reduce disapprovals and platform risk when running **homeowner** roofing offers (inspections, replacements, etc.).  
**Disclaimer:** Meta policies change. Before each major launch, verify against **[Meta for Business](https://www.facebook.com/business)** → Ads / Housing, Employment and Credit resources. This SOP is **not** legal advice.

---

## 1. When this likely applies

If ads promote **housing-related** services to consumers (e.g. **roof work tied to their home**), campaigns are typically run under **Special Ad Category: Housing** (or as Meta directs for your exact creative and landing).

**Do not** run “regular” campaigns with full demographic targeting if the offer is clearly housing-related—risk of **disapproval** or **account restriction**.

---

## 2. Pre-launch checklist (media buyer)

- [ ] Correct **Special Ad Category** selected at **campaign** level  
- [ ] **Geo:** use **allowed** methods (often **radius** from a pin, **city**, etc.—**not** hyper-local practices that violate current rules)  
- [ ] **No prohibited targeting:** do not rely on **age**, **gender**, **ZIP micro-clustering**, or detailed **income** the way you would in non-SAC campaigns  
- [ ] **Creative** does the filtering: storm, insurance, “homeowner,” neighborhood tone—without discriminatory language  
- [ ] **Landing / lead form** claims match ad (no bait-and-switch)  
- [ ] **Lead form** consent text matches **TCPA** / state rules (your `campaign/` form is a starting point—attorney review for production)  
- [ ] **Beneficiary / payer** correctly declared if using client’s Page/ad account

---

## 3. Audiences in SAC

- Prefer **broad geo + strong creative** over tight “interest stacks” that no longer apply.  
- Where Meta offers **Special Ad Audience** / compliant lookalikes, use those per **current** UI—not legacy lookalike workflows.  
- **Retargeting:** follow Meta’s rules for engagement/custom lists in SAC.

---

## 4. If ads are disapproved

1. Screenshot reason in **Account Quality**.  
2. Check **category** + **landing** + **before/after** claims (insurance “100%” style claims often trigger extra scrutiny).  
3. Fix **one** variable; resubmit.  
4. Escalate via **Meta support** with **ad ID**, **policy** line cited, and **plain-language** explanation of the offer.

---

## 5. Client communication

Send clients a **one-liner:**  
“We run your campaigns under Meta’s **Housing** rules, which limit some targeting options compared to 2019-style Facebook ads. We win on **creative, offer, and speed-to-lead**—not on sketchy targeting.”

---

## 6. Internal training

- Re-read Meta’s housing ad guidance **quarterly**.  
- Log **disapproval patterns** in a spreadsheet (creative hook vs landing vs CTA).
