# Key terms to include in your MSA / SOW (not legal advice)

Have a licensed attorney review for your state. This is a **checklist of commercial points** from your 2026 business plan.

**Full merge-ready template:** `legal/CLIENT-SERVICE-AGREEMENT-TEMPLATE.md` (chargeback/refund clauses, guarantee as **account credit**, Exhibits A–B).  
**Research notes:** `legal/RESEARCH-NOTES-chargebacks-refunds.md`

## Parties & scope

- Client = roofing contractor; you = marketing / lead-gen provider
- **Territory:** exclusive while active, defined by ZIPs or map
- **Tier:** Starter ($1,500) / Growth ($2,500) / Domination ($4,500) — attach deck PDF as Exhibit A

## Fees & term

- Monthly retainer, auto-charge date, late payment / pause rules
- **Minimum term** (e.g. 2 months) — explain setup + optimization window
- Ad spend: included vs passed through (be explicit)

## Qualified lead definition

- Match the deck tier guarantee language
- Exclusions: renters, outside territory, obvious spam, duplicate same address within 30 days (if you use this)

## Performance guarantee

- “If fewer than [10 | 20 | 25] qualified leads in first 30 calendar days, month 2 fee is 50% off.” (Starter / Growth / Domination — match printed deck.)
- How counted (your CRM export vs client’s word)
- **Does not** guarantee jobs, revenue, or insurance outcomes

## Client responsibilities

- Call/text leads within agreed SLA
- Accurate licensing, branding, and photo rights for creatives
- No interference with pixel / domain / ad account access

## Your responsibilities

- Deliverables per tier (reports, calls, creative count)
- Compliance: Meta policies, housing/special ad category if applicable in your setup

## Pause / termination

- Notice period, data export, who owns ad accounts and pages
- Final invoice timing

## Liability cap

- Attorney to draft — typically cap at fees paid in last X months; no guarantee of revenue

## Confidentiality & non-disparagement

- Standard mutual clauses as appropriate
